About Me

A student exploring the depths of CyberSecurity and System Internals.

I am a Guy who has focused interest in cybersecurity, system internals, and low-level analysis. My work is driven by curiosity about how systems behave beyond their intended design and how security weaknesses emerge from overlooked assumptions.

I regularly work on CTF challenges involving reverse engineering, binary exploitation, and web security. These challenges help me understand real-world attack surfaces and improve my ability to analyze software at both the application and system level.

My technical background includes Java problem solving, Python scripting, Linux environments, and hands-on debugging and analysis. I enjoy building small tools, experimenting with ideas, and documenting what I learn through notes and writeups.

This space serves as a record of my learning process, experiments, and practical exploration in security and systems.
